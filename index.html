The Importance of Online Security

Cyber threats are constantly evolving. Protect your digital life by understanding common risks and adopting best practices. Remember: security is an ongoing process, not a one-time setup.

  • Phishing: Fake emails or messages designed to steal information
  • Malware: Malicious software that damages or steals data
  • Data Breaches: Unauthorized access to personal or organizational data
  • Social Engineering: Manipulating people into revealing confidential info

Key practices include using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, and keeping your devices updated.

Protect Your Privacy Online

Your privacy is your right. Learn how to control what information you share and with whom. Protect your personal data from being exploited by malicious actors or intrusive companies.

Privacy Tips:

  • Regularly review social media privacy settings
  • Be cautious with sharing personal info publicly
  • Use pseudonyms or aliases when possible
  • Limit app permissions to only what is necessary

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I tell if a message is a scam?

Look for urgent language, suspicious links, and sender details. Always verify with trusted sources before clicking.

How do I make my social media private?

Access your privacy settings on each platform, set your profile to private, and review your audience regularly.

What is two-factor authentication?

It's an extra layer of security requiring a second verification step, like a code sent to your phone, when logging in.

Why should I update my software?

Updates often include security patches that fix vulnerabilities hackers can exploit.
